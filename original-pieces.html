Postcard Art

Water Street Gallery, Todmorden

I created two emulsion transfers of wintery scenes for the Postcard Art call-out at Water Street Gallery, displayed alongside larger pieces from the Seeds of Change exhibition.

I love postcard-sized art because it's accessible, affordable and welcomes a broad range of styles and mediums.

Emulsion transfers can have an ethereal, worn quality to them. For me, they show how photographs can represent faded memories. I think transfers work well as postcard-sized pieces - when the viewer looks closely, they'll start to notice the layers, marks and textures that are created naturally through the process. The outcome of a transfer is unclear until the very end, which helps me to relinquish control of the outcome and enjoy the making process!
